7.
12 + 5X - 3X2 --- Equation 1
X2 - 4 --- Equation 2
What is the sum of the positive root of equation 1 and the negative root of equation 2.

Solution12 + 5X - 3X2 = (12 + 9X)-(4X + 3X2) = 3(4 + 3X) - X(4 + 3X)
X = 3 or -4/3. The positive root is 3.
X2 - 4 = (X + 2)(X - 2)
X = 2 or -2. The negative root is -2.
Therefore, 3 + (-2) = 3 - 2 = 1.

SolutionFirst of all lets evaluate the sum of the first 7 terms of I

10. If 63 mod X is equivalent to 3C0. What is the value of X that ranges from 30 to 40 inclusive.

SolutionFirst of all 3C0 = 3!/0! (3 - 0)! = 1
Therefore, 63 mod X = 1 (63 divided by X leaves a remainder of 1)
The value of X could be 31, 32, 33, 34,..., 39.
Using trial and error method: 63/31 = 2 rem 1.
Therefore, answer is 31.
